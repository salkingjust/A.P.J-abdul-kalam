A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

About A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, widely known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015.

His Achievements

Dr. Kalam made significant contributions to India's space and military technology. He played a key role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He is often referred to as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in the development of ballistic missile technology.

Inspirational Figure

Apart from his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was also a prolific writer and a great motivator. He inspired millions of young minds with his lectures and books, encouraging them to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.
